Conflict

Okonkwo, a respected man in the Village of Umuofia, who is indecisive in his own beliefs and societ'al standards. The conflict in the novel is the traditional customs of the Igbo and the new customs brought by colonizers. "And then after another lifetime these men opened the caves again and the locusts came to Umuofia.". The locusts, the white colonists, supressed the villagers faith.

Theme

One major theme in the novel is pride. Throughout the novel, Okonkwo trys to build his status in society and maintain personal pride in his success. He does this trough many different actions. One way he builds pride is through growing yams. Yams represent masculinity which is very important to Okonkwo. Another way he builds his pride is through becoming an Egwugwu. Egwugwu are respected and becomeing one means you are a respected man in the village.
